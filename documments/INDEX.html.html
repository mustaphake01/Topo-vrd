Welcome to tinghir

Tinghir is an oasis about 30 kilometres (19 mi) long and about 4 kilometres (2.5 mi) wide.
The climate is arid subtropical: hot, dry winters about altitude (1,430 metres (4,690 ft)).
There are a few rainy days per year,
with the greatest precipitation in fall and winter. The Tinghir region is wedged between two mountain ranges,
stretching over 700 kilometres430 mi)southwest to northeast Morocco:
the High Atlas in the north, with a high peak of over 4,167 metres (13,671 ft),and the Little Atlas in
the southThe road from Ouarzazate to Imtghren parallels the mountains.During the Mesozoic,
the region was invaded by the sea, where thick deposits of sediments rich in marine fossils
(particularly of the ammonitida class).

Tinghir Travel Guide

Tinghir is a small but remarkable town located in southern Morocco,
known for its stunning landscapes and rich cultural heritage.
Nestled between the High Atlas Mountains and the Sahara Desert,
it serves as a gateway for travelers looking to explore the
natural beauty of the region.
This town is not just a stopover but a destination
filled with adventure,history, and tradition.
From lush palm groves to towering cliffs,
Tinghir promises unforgettable
experiences for all types of travelers.
Adventure seekers can trek through the famous Todgha Gorge,
while those interested in culture can immerse
themselves in the vibrant Berber traditions. Tinghir’s
unique blend of natural beauty and cultural richness
makes it a must-visit for anyone traveling through Morocco.
